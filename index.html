Lesson 1: Project Management Foundations

This lesson introduces the foundational distinctions and definitions used throughout the PMBOK® Guide, Eighth Edition.

Projects

Projects are temporary. They have a defined beginning and end and are initiated to produce tangible or intangible deliverables, including products, services, or other results. A project's deliverables may continue to provide benefits after the project ends.

Projects vs. Operations

Organizational work includes both projects and operations. Projects are temporary and help create change. Operations are ongoing and sustain products, services, and business functions.

Value

Value includes financial and nonfinancial benefits in excess of the investment. Different stakeholders may perceive value differently, so value can be expressed quantitatively or qualitatively.

Project Success

Project success is broader than cost, schedule, and scope performance. It reflects whether beneficiaries, stakeholders, and participants believe the project delivered value worth the effort and expense.

Programs and Portfolios

Programs coordinate related projects to obtain combined benefits. Portfolios group programs, projects, and operations to achieve strategic objectives and maximize value.

Governance

Project governance guides decisions, authority, oversight, alignment, and change. The governance approach should be tailored to the organization, project context, industry, and delivery approach.

Exam mindset: When several answers seem reasonable, favor the response that protects value, engages the appropriate stakeholders, supports strategic alignment, and adapts responsibly to the project context.
