LESSON 1 • FOUNDATION

Project Management Foundations

Build the vocabulary and decision-making mindset used throughout the PMBOK® Guide, Eighth Edition.

Lesson outcome: Distinguish projects from operations, explain value and success, understand programs and portfolios, and recognize how governance and roles guide decisions.

1. Projects

A project is temporary and is undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result. Temporary means the work has a defined beginning and end. The benefits of a project's deliverables may continue long after the project closes.

Remember: temporary does not mean short.

2. Projects and Operations

Projects create change and unique outcomes. Operations sustain ongoing products, services, and business functions. Projects often transfer deliverables, knowledge, and resources to operations.

Exam tip: involve operations early when they will support the delivered outcome.

3. Value

Value includes financial and nonfinancial benefits compared with the investment used to achieve them. Different stakeholders may perceive value differently, so value can be quantitative or qualitative.

Ask: did the work create benefits worth the investment?

4. Project Success

Success is broader than meeting scope, schedule, and cost targets. It includes whether beneficiaries, stakeholders, and participants believe the project delivered worthwhile value.

Exam tip: clarify success criteria with stakeholders early.

5. Programs and Portfolios

A program coordinates related projects to obtain combined benefits. A portfolio groups programs, projects, and operations to achieve strategic objectives and maximize value.

Portfolio → Program → Project.

6. Governance

Governance establishes authority, accountability, oversight, decision rights, and escalation paths. It should be tailored to the organization, industry, project complexity, and development approach.

Exam tip: too much governance creates waste; too little creates alignment and performance problems.

7. Roles and Stakeholders

Project management responsibilities may be performed by different roles depending on context. Stakeholders include anyone who can affect, be affected by, or perceive themselves to be affected by the project.

The function matters more than the title.

8. The PMI Decision Mindset

When several choices appear reasonable, prefer the one that protects value, engages the appropriate stakeholders, follows governance, uses the correct authority, and adapts responsibly to context.

Look for FIRST, NEXT, BEST, and MOST appropriate in question wording.

Lesson 1 summary

  • Projects are temporary and unique; operations are ongoing.
  • Value and stakeholder outcomes matter when judging success.
  • Programs coordinate related projects; portfolios align work with strategy.
  • Governance defines authority and should be tailored.
  • Project management functions may be performed by different roles.
